El Niño
What is it?
El Niño Southern Oscillation, aka ENSO, is a cycle of warm (El Niño) and cool (La Niña) weather events that occur every few years in the equatorial Pacific Ocean.
El Niño and California
In California, an El Niño event results in unusally wet weather in traditionally dry Southern California, and unusually dry weather in traditionally wet Northern California. However, it has global impacts, vastly altering climates from North America, to Southeast Asia, and all the way to South Africa.
What causes El Niño?
El Niño is caused by a reversal of the trade winds in the equatorial Pacific Ocean, off the coast of Ecuador. This reversal causes the upwelling of cold water along the coast, creating a large temperature sink, which eventually deflects the global jet stream.
A Global Phenomenon
The El Niño cycle "...is the most dramatic year-to-year variation of the Earth’s climate system, affecting agriculture, public health, freshwater availability, power generation, and economic activity in the United States and around the globe."
La Niña
La Niña is the cool phase of the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO), characterized by cooler-than-average sea surface temperatures in the central and eastern equatorial Pacific Ocean, which impacts global weather patterns
The Human Impact
The impact of human induced climate change will increase the extremes, on both ends, of future El Niño and La Niña events.
